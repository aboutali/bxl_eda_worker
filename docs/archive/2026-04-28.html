Today

Brussels adopted its 20th sanctions package against Russia, hitting energy revenues, the military-industrial base, trade, financial services and crypto — a measure Bern will need to assess for autonomous alignment under the standard Embargo Act process. In parallel, the Council finalised a €90 billion Macro-Financial Assistance loan to Kyiv to be serviced from proceeds on immobilised Russian sovereign assets, while Costa, von der Leyen and Zelenskyy issued a joint statement reaffirming EU-Ukraine alignment; Switzerland's position on the immobilised-assets mechanism remains a live question given its financial-centre exposure. The Council also rolled over Myanmar restrictive measures to 30 April 2027, another file Bern typically tracks. On the wider foreign-policy agenda, HR/VP Kallas marked three years of war in Sudan with calls for ceasefire and humanitarian access — a potential co-sign opportunity — opened the 25th EU-ASEAN Ministerial in Brussels, and the EU flagged the EPC and EU-Armenia summit set for 4-5 May 2026. In the Middle East, Iranian FM Araghchi met Putin and Lavrov, blaming Washington for the collapse of nuclear talks, as SRF reporting drew attention to Switzerland's non-shared fuel reserves and the limits of European energy solidarity.
