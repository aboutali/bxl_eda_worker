Today

Trump's declaration that the US-Iran war has "terminated" — timed to the 60-day War Powers deadline and aimed at bypassing congressional authorization — dominated the day, with Tehran's foreign minister setting preconditions for any follow-on talks and SRF assessing Trump faces no domestic check on the unauthorized strikes. The Iran file is bleeding into transatlantic relations: the Pentagon ordered withdrawal of 5,000 US troops from Germany amid Trump-Merz friction, and Trump's renewed threat of higher tariffs on EU cars — rejected by Brussels — is being read in EU capitals as either process frustration or Iran-linked pressure, a dynamic Bern should track given exposure on both trade and any forthcoming Iran sanctions alignment. At an informal Cyprus summit, EU leaders reaffirmed Middle East partnerships and Ukraine support, while Kallas convened the Nordic-Baltic Eight in Kuressaare and the bloc held its first summit with Armenia in Yerevan, signaling a tightening hawkish core and accelerated hedging against Washington. Operationally, the EU disbursed €45 million to Lebanon's social ministry and broadened sectoral cooperation with Beirut, issued a fresh Ukraine statement, and Costa spoke with Sheinbaum — items Swiss services should monitor for co-signature or alignment opportunities. Lower-tier signals worth flagging: NZZ reporting that Iran's IRGC emerged strengthened and Iraq destabilized, and Sybiha's push to bar Russia from the Venice Biennale after the jury's mass resignation.
