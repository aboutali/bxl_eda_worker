Today

Iran tensions dominated, with Trump launching US Navy "Project Freedom" to escort commercial shipping through the Strait of Hormuz after Iranian cruise missiles reportedly struck the UAE — the first such attack since the ceasefire — followed by an exchange of fire between US and Iranian vessels that has stalled Hormuz traffic and rattled the truce; Trump separately signalled he will reject Tehran's latest proposal. The EEAS condemned the Iranian strikes on neighbours and called for restraint, a line Bern will likely find consonant with its own messaging, while EU finance ministers split over how to absorb the energy-price shock, with the Commission and presidency leaning on the IMF to block untargeted relief. In parallel, the inaugural EU-Armenia summit in Yerevan produced a connectivity partnership and deeper security and economic ties, with Canadian PM Carney joining to frame Europe as the anchor of a rebuilt order against Russian and US pressure; Kallas will follow up with visits to Yerevan and Baku. On Gaza, Brussels criticised Israel's advance of the "yellow line" to roughly 60% of the enclave and reduced aid flows, with Swiss reporting echoing concerns that Hamas elites are capturing diverted supplies as reconstruction stalls.
