Marking two years since Russia's invasion, the EU's fifteenth-anniversary package targets 74 shadow-fleet tankers and, notably, three Dubai-based insurers, paired with a new sanctions-envoy mandate responding to evidence of €4bn in dual-use goods transiting Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan—an enforcement turn mirrored in Bern, where Brunner's listings of 19 individuals and 8 entities linked to Shahed production (including two Hong Kong fronts) and the first EU-CH bilateral since January's alignment package signal tighter circumvention focus, even as Brussels probes Russian-gold provenance flows through Ticino and Valais refineries.
