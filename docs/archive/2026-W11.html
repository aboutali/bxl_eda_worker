Today

EU foreign ministers adopted a sweeping anti-circumvention listing under the oil price cap, designating 74 vessels alongside three Indian refining intermediaries and a Dubai-based marine insurer — a package Bundesrat Cassis discussed aligning with in talks on Swiss participation in the EU's circumvention task force, even as reporting surfaced a Geneva commodities financier allegedly channelling €280m in Belaruskali receivables through Cypriot SPVs and fresh allegations that Swiss-made components reached Israeli and Emirati end-users via German and Italian conduits. On the Middle East track, Kallas co-chaired a Cairo-PA donor session conditioning disbursements on governance reforms and Rafah oversight, while a parliamentary subcommittee pressed the EEAS to consult the E3 on JCPOA snapback should Iranian executions continue — a trajectory analysts warn is narrowing the space for Switzerland's protecting-power mandate, with some urging the E3 to build humanitarian carve-outs and an explicit protecting-power channel into any snapback sequencing. Separately, the Council President extracted Turkish migration commitments against Commission negotiating directives due in June, and Šefčovič unveiled a Central Asia critical-raw-materials package spanning tungsten, rare earths and lithium with Kazakhstan, Uzbekistan and Mongolia. Bern's exposure is sharpening on three fronts simultaneously — sanctions alignment, export-control credibility, and the viability of its Tehran good-offices role.
