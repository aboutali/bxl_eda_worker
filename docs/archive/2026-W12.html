Today

The Foreign Affairs Council adopted its broadest shadow-fleet package to date, listing 74 vessels, three Indian refiners and a Dubai-based marine insurance broker, alongside a phased ban on Russian LNG ship-to-ship transfers in EU waters; SECO has signalled an autonomous alignment within a fortnight, with a Geneva-domiciled subsidiary of a Singaporean trader caught in the perimeter and FDP voices pressing for clarity on Zurich-linked insurance entries, even as analysts flag Swiss and Hong Kong intermediaries as the likely arbitrage channel. In parallel, Šefčovič tabled a targeted Article 2 review of the EU-Israel agreement covering settlement-origin goods, backed by HR/VP messaging on differentiation and a draft leaders' invitation foreshadowing debate on Israel, Mali and a possible Mauritania mission — prompting several European ambassadors in Bern to sound out the EDA on partial-suspension alignment, a politically harder ask than Russia tracking. Hungary's carve-out on nuclear cooperation lines greased FAC adoption but leaves the unanimity problem intact for the next round.
