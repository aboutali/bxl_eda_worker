Today

Brussels' 21st sanctions package—74 shadow-fleet vessels, 12 maritime insurers and tightened tech curbs—now sits on the Federal Council's desk, with SECO signalling that the insurance provisions raise the thorniest transposition questions and an EEAS evidentiary annex reportedly naming two Geneva commodities traders for price-cap circumvention, sharpening pressure on Bern to align without carve-outs. In parallel, Šefčovič's regulation routing €3.2bn of Euroclear-held interest to a Ukraine recovery facility advances the windfall-transfer track that Swiss legal services are separately stress-testing against sovereign-immunity and market-stability constraints, a debate the FM-HR/VP meeting on the peace formula, sanctions alignment and Bilaterals III is expected to touch directly. Cohesion strains are visible elsewhere: Nicosia's quiet removal of two designations citing evidentiary gaps has reopened the unanimity question, while Baltic-Hungarian divisions over Georgia visa suspension hint at a fragmenting CFSP consensus. On the southern flank, the killing of three UNIFIL peacekeepers—two European—and fresh G7-shared intelligence on a Tehran–Sana'a weapons pipeline are converging toward new designations at the 14 April FAC, with Swiss co-signature of the joint statement notable. A non-binding 42-28 committee vote urging Article 2 review of the Israel association agreement adds political, if not yet operational, weight to the Gaza file.
