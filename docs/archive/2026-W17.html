Today

Brussels tightened the Russia-sanctions screw on two fronts: the EU added 47 vessels and seven P&I intermediaries to its price-cap evasion list, while the Home Affairs Commissioner stood up a 60-officer circumvention unit aimed at third-country trans-shipment — moves that have prompted Bern to weigh faster alignment with EU listings to contain reputational exposure in the commodities sector, with several member states separately pressing Switzerland to join the common dual-use surveillance mechanism covering free-port transit. On the Middle East, HR/VP Kallas told ministers humanitarian access remained "wholly inadequate" and floated targeted measures against financiers of settler violence, themes the EDA chief took up bilaterally with her on sanctions coordination and Gaza access. A Council-commissioned working paper came down in favour of a 100% windfall tax over outright confiscation of immobilised Russian assets, narrowing the legal options ahead of June. Secondary tracks included a push to trigger the Anti-Coercion Instrument over Chinese gallium and germanium curbs, a recalibration of Africa engagement toward the coastal west after the Sahel CSDP drawdown, and a WB6 enlargement summit on 6 May.
