Today

Brussels adopted its 20th sanctions package against Russia, hitting energy revenues, the military-industrial complex, trade, financial services and crypto — a decision Bern will need to assess for autonomous alignment under the Embargo Act, alongside the Council's parallel finalisation of a €90 billion Macro-Financial Assistance loan to Ukraine to be serviced from immobilised Russian sovereign assets. The Ukraine track was reinforced politically by a joint Costa–von der Leyen–Zelenskyy statement on continued EU support, a text Switzerland may face pressure to echo. On the Middle East, Iran reportedly tabled a new proposal to Washington while FM Araghchi travelled to Moscow blaming the US for the collapse of talks; EU capitals are simultaneously scrambling to cushion energy-price and growth fallout with thinner fiscal margins than in 2022, and SRF reporting on Swiss-only fuel reserves raises an energy-solidarity question Bern will likely have to answer. Secondary Council business included a one-year rollover of Myanmar restrictive measures to 30 April 2027 (relevant for Swiss alignment) and a Kallas statement marking three years of war in Sudan paired with a new EU mine-action programme there.
